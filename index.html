ABOUT

Muhammed Ali

Muhammad Ali was an American professional boxer and activist. Nicknamed "the Greatest", he is regarded as one of the most significant sports figures of the 20th century and is often regarded as the greatest heavyweight boxer of all time. He held the Ring magazine heavyweight title from 1964 Born and raised in Louisville, Kentucky, he began training as an amateur boxer at age 12. At 18, he won a gold medal in the light heavyweight division at the 1960 Summer Olympics and turned professional later that year.

DRILLS

MENTALITY

From an early age, Ali found the physical endurance and discipline to become a world champion: roadwork, gym work, sparring, diet, positive mindset, and putting his dreams first. Ali's life path led him to respecting self and others, his purpose and God.

ENDURENCE

Ali trained spiritually, mentally and physically, swimming and running was two training methods that allowed him to maneuver around his opponents with endurance and confidence allowing him the ability to visualize his victory, like a prophesies, he new he was a born warrior and trained as one.

CALISTHENICS

Strength Training and Calisthenics. Sit-Ups and Push-Ups: Ali performed approximately 500 sit-ups a day along with numerous push-ups, sculpting his core strength and overall muscle endurance.

GYM

Muhammad Ali started his training at the Columbia Gym, located on South Fourth Street in Louisville. There, he dedicated himself to rigorous training sessions every day. He engaged in intense sparring sessions, jumping rope, using punching bags, and perfecting his movements through shadowboxing.
